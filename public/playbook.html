Host Journey

From first contact to live listing — complete flow for property owners and landlords

Ideal Host Profile

  • Owns or manages commercial space in Boston metro
  • Has vacant or underutilized space (retail, office, flex, light industrial)
  • Is a legal entity (LLC, Corp, Partnership) — not an individual sole proprietor
  • Has authority to sublicense (fee owner or written landlord consent)
  • Looking for 30-day+ occupancy, not hourly/event bookings

Disqualifying Factors

  • Individual (not a business entity) — increases classification risk
  • Cannot confirm landlord consent to sublicense
  • Space has known zoning violations or open ISD complaints
  • Asking for security deposits outside the platform
  • Space is residential — Trulo is commercial only
1
Outreach & Qualification Call
Make contact via direct mail campaign, LinkedIn, or inbound lead. On the discovery call, collect:
  • Property details: address, square footage, current use, zoning classification
  • Ownership: do they own outright or are they a sub-landlord? If sub-landlord, do they have written consent to sublicense?
  • Entity: what legal entity holds the property? (Need LLC/Corp name and state of formation)
  • Asking rate: monthly License Fee they want to charge
  • Availability: when can it be listed? Any existing tenant obligations?
Discovery
2
Send Host Listing Agreement via PandaDoc
Before any listing is created or any data is collected, the Host must sign the Host Listing Agreement. This document covers:
  • Trulo's 10% Host Fee
  • Superior Landlord Consent representation + mandatory checkbox
  • Platform rules, listing standards, and cancellation obligations
  • Indemnification of Trulo for sublicensing authority failures
  • Tax obligations and W-9/TIN submission requirement
Send via PandaDoc. Do not proceed to Step 3 until you have a signed copy.

  • Master Terms of Service — platform-wide rules, arbitration clause, liability limits

  • E-Sign Disclosure & Consent — legally validates all electronic signatures

  • Individuals (if approved): provide SSN

Tenant Journey

From first contact to approved booking — complete flow for businesses seeking space

Ideal Tenant Profile

  • Early-stage brand, startup, or creator needing commercial space
  • Business entity (LLC, Corp, sole proprietor with EIN)
  • Needs 30+ days of dedicated commercial space
  • Has or can obtain commercial general liability insurance
  • Boston area or relocating to Boston metro

Use Cases We Accept

  • Pop-up retail / brand activation
  • Creative studio / production space
  • Office / co-working (dedicated, not shared desk)
  • Light warehouse / fulfillment
  • Showroom / gallery
  • Food production (zoning-dependent — must verify)

Booking Lifecycle

From booking confirmation through occupancy to termination
Booking Confirmed
Commercial License Agreement Executed
The moment a Host accepts a booking request, the Commercial License Agreement is generated and sent via PandaDoc to both Host and Tenant. Both parties must sign before any funds are charged.

What the CLA covers: Space details, monthly License Fee, start date, 30-day protected period, 30-day rolling notice, permitted uses, zoning classification, ADA disclosure, no security deposit clause, insurance requirements, holdover consequences (150% rate), and dispute resolution (AAA arbitration, Boston MA).
Before Occupancy Begins
COI Collection + Payment Authorization
Two things must be complete before Day 1:

1. Certificate of Insurance (COI) — Tenant must upload or email a COI showing commercial general liability insurance meeting Trulo's requirements. Trulo and Host must both be named as additional insured. Do not allow occupancy without a valid COI on file.

2. First Month's Payment — Stripe charges the Tenant's payment method for Month 1. Amount = Monthly License Fee + 2% Tenant Service Fee. Funds held by Trulo until Day 7 of occupancy.
Day 1 — Occupancy Begins
License Period Starts. 30-Day Protected Period Begins.
Tenant has access to the Space. The 30-day protected period is now running. Neither Host nor Tenant may issue a termination notice during this period. The License Fee clock starts.
Day 7
First Payout Released to Host
After 7 days of confirmed occupancy without dispute, Trulo releases Month 1 funds to Host via Stripe Connect.

Host receives: Monthly License Fee × 90%
Trulo retains: 10% Host Fee + 2% Tenant Service Fee

If a legitimate dispute is filed within the first 7 days, funds are held pending resolution.
Monthly (Recurring)
Automatic Monthly Billing Cycle
On each monthly anniversary of the start date, Stripe automatically charges Tenant and releases funds to Host within 7 days. No manual action required once the booking is active on the platform.
After Day 30
Rolling Notice Period Opens
Either party may now issue a 30-day written notice of termination. Notice must be submitted in writing through the platform (or via email to legal@jointrulo.com during manual phase). The License continues through the end of the 30-day notice period — partial months are pro-rated.
Termination Date
Move-Out + Final Billing
Tenant vacates the Space on or before the effective termination date. Final month is pro-rated. Host confirms space is in acceptable condition. Final payout released to Host within 7 days of termination date, less any damage claims submitted through the platform.
Holdover Warning: If Tenant remains in the Space after the effective termination date without Host's written consent, a holdover fee of 150% of the monthly License Fee applies for each month or partial month of unauthorized occupancy. Trulo may immediately suspend the Tenant's platform access and withhold pending payments. Do not waive this provision without legal counsel.

Insurance Requirements

Current policy (Tenant-provided COI) + future embedded product
Current Policy — Tenant Brings Own COI
1
Tenant must provide a Certificate of Insurance (COI) before Day 1
Minimum coverage requirements (confirm exact amounts with your lawyer):
  • Commercial General Liability: $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate (recommended)
  • Additional Insured: Both Trulo Corporation AND the Host entity must be named as additional insured on the policy
  • Policy term: Must be active through at least the first 30-day protected period; ideally the full anticipated occupancy
  • Carrier: Admitted carrier in Massachusetts
2
For High-Risk Uses: Additional Coverage Required
If Tenant's use involves food production, manufacturing, events with public attendees, or other elevated risk uses, they must also provide:
  • Product liability coverage (if applicable)
  • Liquor liability (if serving alcohol at events)
  • Workers' compensation if they will have employees on site
High-Risk Use Addendum must also be executed.
3
What to tell Tenants who don't have insurance
Direct them to: Thimble (thimble.com) for on-demand commercial GL — policies available by the month. Most affordable and fastest option for early-stage tenants. Also: Hiscox or Next Insurance for small business GL. Do not tell them to use personal or renters insurance — these do not cover commercial use.
Future State — Embedded Insurance Product

When Trulo's embedded insurance partner is live, the flow will change: instead of collecting a Tenant COI, Trulo will offer insurance at checkout — purchased by the Tenant as part of the booking flow at a per-month rate. The COI requirement will remain as a fallback option for Tenants with existing commercial policies. Host protection coverage (similar to Airbnb's AirCover) may also become available as a competitive differentiator.

Note: Any embedded insurance product requires Massachusetts insurance producer licensing review before launch. Do not offer or market insurance without counsel sign-off.

Ongoing Account Management

What Kourosh and Elliott do after a deal closes
W1
Week 1 Check-In (Both Parties)
Email both Host and Tenant on Day 3 and Day 7. Confirm:
  • Tenant has access to the space and no issues with entry, utilities, or condition
  • Host has no concerns about Tenant's use
  • COI is on file and valid
  • Stripe payment processed successfully
Day 7 payout to Host triggers automatically — confirm Host receives it.
Both Parties
M1
Month 1 Milestone Check
At the end of the first month (Day 30), the protected period ends. Call both parties:
  • Is the relationship working? Any concerns?
  • Does either party anticipate needing to terminate?
  • Is Tenant's intended use still the same as at booking? (Flag use changes — may require amended CLA)
  • Is COI still active? Will it need to be renewed?
Both Parties
MR
Monthly Recurring — Payment Monitoring
Each month, verify:
  • Stripe charge succeeded — if a payment fails, notify both parties immediately. License is suspended until payment is resolved.
  • Host payout was released on schedule (7 days after charge)
  • No open disputes or damage claims
Recurring
TN
Termination Notice Received
When either party issues a 30-day notice:
  • Log the date notice was received — this is Day 0 of the 30-day notice period
  • Confirm the effective termination date in writing to both parties
  • Notify the other party immediately
  • Begin re-listing process with Host (don't wait until the last day)
  • Confirm Tenant move-out plan and final billing amount

Escalation Paths

What to do when things go wrong
Situation Immediate Action Contract Basis
Tenant fails to pay Stripe retry triggers automatically. If retry fails after 3 attempts, suspend Tenant's platform access and notify Host. Issue written cure notice to Tenant — 5 business days to resolve. If unresolved, initiate termination. Master Terms + Commercial License Agreement
Tenant overholds (doesn't move out) Immediately activate holdover billing at 150%. Issue written notice to Tenant that they are in holdover. Do not advise Host to physically remove Tenant — that requires court process. Escalate to legal counsel. Commercial License Agreement §5.5
Host's superior landlord revokes consent Host must notify Trulo within 24 hours. This is an involuntary Host cancellation — 30% cancellation penalty applies. Trulo must find Tenant alternative space or refund remaining prepaid fees within 5 days. Host Listing Agreement — Superior Landlord Consent section
Property damage claim Document with photos within 24 hours of Tenant vacating. File damage claim through platform. Tenant's COI is the first line of recovery. For claims below insurance deductible, follow Damage Claims Policy (Doc 11). Never withhold a payout without formal claim filed. Damage Claims Policy (Doc 11) + Tenant's COI
Dispute between Host and Tenant Both parties must attempt good-faith resolution within 30 days (Dispute Resolution Policy, Doc 8). If unresolved, refer to AAA Commercial Arbitration, Boston MA. Trulo does not adjudicate disputes — provide the arbitration filing information and step aside. Dispute Resolution Policy + Master Terms §17
Host or Tenant alleges discrimination Do not dismiss. Document the complaint in writing immediately. Review Non-Discrimination Policy (Doc 12). Escalate to legal counsel within 24 hours. Preserve all platform communications. Non-Discrimination Policy (Doc 12) + FHA
Potential zoning violation If you become aware that Tenant's use may violate the Space's Boston zoning classification, notify Tenant in writing immediately. Tenant has sole responsibility for zoning compliance. Do not provide legal advice. Recommend Tenant consult the Boston Inspectional Services Department (ISD). Tenant User Agreement + Zoning Disclosure Policy
Data breach or security incident Activate Incident Response Plan. Notify MA AG and affected users without unreasonable delay (M.G.L. c. 93H). Do not downplay or delay. Escalate to legal counsel immediately. WISP + Incident Response Plan (internal)
Standing Rule: Any situation involving a legal threat, regulatory inquiry, potential lawsuit, or press inquiry goes to Kourosh immediately — do not respond, make promises, or take action without Kourosh's direct sign-off. When in doubt, the answer is: "Let me look into this and get back to you within 24 hours."
